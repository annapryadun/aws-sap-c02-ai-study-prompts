
# Round 1: Keyword Identification

## Objective
Train the ability to rapidly extract relevant information from verbose scenarios.

## Why This Matters
SAP-C02 questions are intentionally verbose — packed with realistic but irrelevant details designed to simulate real-world ambiguity. Most of the text is context padding. This round trains you to cut through the noise and identify only what drives the correct answer.

## Prompt

Copy everything between the two horizontal lines (---) below and paste it into Kiro after the setup prompt:

---

Let's begin Round 1: Keyword Identification.

Generate an exam-style SAP-C02 question (scenario only, no answer options yet). Make it verbose with realistic but irrelevant context padding, as the real exam does. The question should be 150–250 words and include:
- A realistic company scenario with industry context (filler)
- Technical architecture details (some relevant, some filler)
- A specific problem or requirement
- Constraints that narrow the solution space
- A clear question at the end

After I identify what I believe are the keywords and constraints, reveal the actual keywords in **bold** and explain:
1. Which keywords I correctly identified
2. Which ones I missed and why they matter
3. Which details I flagged that are actually irrelevant filler

Generate one question at a time. After feedback, proceed to the next question. Track my accuracy across 10 questions, then summarize my keyword identification patterns.

Focus areas for keyword generation:
- Constraint phrases: "without replacing," "minimal operational overhead," "most cost-effective," "least amount of changes"
- Architecture components: specific services, configurations, and integrations mentioned
- Problem indicators: error codes, performance issues, scaling challenges
- Requirements: compliance needs, latency targets, availability requirements

---

⚠️ **Everything below this point is for your reference only — do not paste into Kiro.**

## How to Practice

1. Read the generated scenario carefully
2. Write down your identified keywords (aim for 5–10 per question)
3. Categorize them: **Constraints** | **Architecture** | **Problem** | **Goal**
4. Submit your list to Kiro for feedback
5. Review what you missed and adjust your scanning technique

## Success Criteria
- Consistently identifying 80%+ of relevant keywords
- Correctly filtering out narrative filler
- Completing keyword extraction in under 30 seconds per question
